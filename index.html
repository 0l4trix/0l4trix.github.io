After the bombing

Probably everyone knows the tragic story of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. In early August 1945, the United States dropped two atomic bombs on Japan, devastating these two cities. More than 100,000 people ended up dying either from direct exposure or the long-term side effects of radiation such as leukemia or cancer.

However, as time passed, these cities started to 'heal' themselves, becoming once more the full of life places they had been, serving as a reminder of the human ability to regenrate. Here's how the bomb sites of these two cities look today.
