You are on a high mountainside, close to the top. You have nothing on you but the clothes on your back. Looking down all you see is the grass-covered foot of the mountain, rocky surfaces and a river. Good luck not only getting down alive but finding people who might help your lost self (Tip: You can move with the buttons below after pressing 'Continue')

The path is too steep here. You can't continue this way (unless you want to die a rather gruesome death, which you don't, believe me)

You see something glistening in the water. You lean down to see what it is.

Oh look! You found a (somewhat rusty) key! Seems like your little detour wasn't for nothing. Take it?

Good choice! It might prove to be useful later.

Are you kidding? You have absolutely nothing on you! You're not in a position to be picky right now. Take the damned key!

Are you planning to climb back on the mountain? If yes, there are better, more traversable places for that.

Seriously why are you walking this way? There's nothing here. Or are you going to drown yourself?

You start to make your way down the southern part of the mountain (at least you assume it is the southern part). Soon you find yourself facing a huge drop. It's obvious that you can't continue in this direction.

You climb down the somewhat-less-steep side of the mountain, careful not to slip and fall to your demise.

You continue on regardless. You're knee-deep in the water now. Maybe you should turn back.

You try going south anyway despite how dangerous it looks. You slip and die. Unexpected, isn't it?

You find yourself face-to-face with a sphinx. She tells you that if you can guess the answer correctly to the riddle she's going to tell you, she'll let you live. Graciously, she'll even give you not one but two chances to get it right (meaning you can answer incorrectly once).

She looks surprised and a bit disappointed but, true to her word, lets you go. More accurately she leaves herself, flying south.

There's nothing here apart from a few goats.

The surface you've been walking on is becoming scarce in this direction. You don't see anything but the ocean in the distance either.

You're on a slope leading down the mountain. There's nothing interesting here, you should keeping looking for a way out.

Still nothing around.

Your standing on the river's bank. It's too deep here to cross though. You'll have to find another way through.

You see a brown rabbit. It also sees you, freezing. As you take a step towards it however, it flees.

The river look somewhat shallow here compared to rest of it. This place is probably your best bet at crossing it.

You see a berrybush in the distance.

Now that you're close enough to examine it properly, you see that these berries are deep blue in colour with small, ominious bloodred patches on them. Eat some?

You tenderly pick one, popping it into your mouth. It's really tasty! You eat some more to satisfy your hunger then decide it's time to move on (hopefully you won't end up keeling over later from this dubious decision of yours)

You don't touch the berries. It's probably for the best.

With your luck, you end up running into the sphinx again. Maybe you shouldn't have followed in the direction she left. Anyway, here we go again.

Now she looks not only disappointed but kinda angry too. More importantly, she's not moving an inch from where she's been perching. You decide it would be better to leave yourself. You start to slowly back away, not looking away from her. She also keeps watching you all the while, but you get away.

There's nothing here.

You realise that you are standing right next to a gigantic dragon, only separated by the river. How comes you didn't notice it before?! Admittedly, it is a grey-blue colour like everything else around but still. Thank god it's asleep at least otherwise you would have become dinner already. Talking about dinner, it's getting a bit late. Maybe you should try to find that exit already.

You continue quietly sneaking away from the dragon and that's when you notice it in the distance. The silhouette of a village! You probably could have noticed it earlier but because of the dragon and chest you were a bit too occupied. You won did it! Congrats! (It's a shame you didn't get to open the chest though)

You find a glistening treasure chest!

Open it?

You open the chest and find... a gorgeous but deadly-looking sword! Who puts a weapon in a treasure chest? You take it out but not a moment sooner do you see a huge shadow falling over you. Looks like the opening of the chest woke the dragon up. It shreds you to pieces so fast you don't even get to think of defending yourself with your newfound weapon.

You continue on. Though the only way forward is to the south-west because south from here the rocky surface seems to disappear in favour of the deep water.

You died

You've already been here. Nothing has changed since then. You're welcome to keep trying though, it's not MY time you're wasting after all.
